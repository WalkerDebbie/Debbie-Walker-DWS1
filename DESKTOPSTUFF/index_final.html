Fun facts

Golden prefer the company of people rather than other animals. It's no wonder why Golden Retrievers are the third most common dog in the United States. The other reasons are because they are smart, loyal and easily trained.

50 Shades of Golden

The original cross was of a yellow-coloured retriever, 'Nous', with a Tweed Water Spaniel female dog, 'Belle'. The Tweed Water Spaniel is now extinct, but was then common in the border country. Marjoribanks had purchased Nous in 1865 from an unregistered litter of otherwise black wavy-coated retriever pups. In 1868, this cross produced a litter that included four pups; these four became the basis of a breeding program which included the Irish Setter, the sandy-coloured Bloodhound, the St. John's water dog of Newfoundland, and two more wavy-coated black retrievers. The bloodline was also inbred and selected for trueness to Marjoribanks' idea of the ultimate hunting dog. His vision included a more vigorous and powerful dog than previous retrievers, one that would still be gentle and trainable. Russian sheepdogs are not mentioned in these records, nor are any other working dog breeds. The ancestry of the Golden Retriever is all sporting dogs, in line with Marjoribanks' goals. The Golden Retriever was active and powerful and had a gentle mouth for retrieving games while on hunts.

A Dog's Life

Finally the day you've been waiting for! You're bringing home a new member to your family, but wait. Mike says, There are a few things you should do before, during and after:

Before you bring your Golden home:

What to buy first.

  • Purchase metal or porcelain bowls only - plastic bowls can trap bacteria in scratches of the plastic - you'll need two (one for food and one for water)
  • Schedule an appointment with a local veterinarian clinic - Mike says it's a good idea to ask a dog-loving friend make a recommendation

Pet proof your house!

  1. Make sure to hide electrical cords
  2. Put toilet seats down
  3. Install safety locks on cabinets that have poisons stored in them
  4. Save your furniture and shoes - purchase a few chew toys - find durable toys without buttons or strings that your dog can chew off and get caught in his/her throat

Debbie says, Puppies (and some very curious older dogs - not me of course) can be like small children. When bored, they'll find ways to entertain themselves - which usually leads to something being destroyed.

On your way home:

Visit the veterinarian...

For Puppies:

  1. Have vaccines administered
  2. Check-up, for worms

For Adults:

  1. Tick/flea prescriptions
  2. Spade or neutered

After your Golden settles in:

  • Make sure to keep his/her teeth cleaned
  • Frequently groom
  • Regularly exercise
  • Love and adore
